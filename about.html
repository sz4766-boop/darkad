Purpose & scope

This is a small, professionally oriented website that presents Ironmace as a case study for how a small game studio can benefit from clear security communication. I describe common anti‑cheat concepts and high‑level mitigation patterns, informed by previous collaboration with an anti‑cheat group on reducing abuse vectors.

Current: 1.6

Discord collaboration (evidence)

Part of my experience came from communicating with Ironmace’s anti‑cheat administrators in a Discord server. The goal of these conversations was not to discuss exploit development, but to coordinate on responsible reporting and practical mitigation. Typical work included: clarifying reproduction steps, identifying which trust boundary was failing (client/network/server), and confirming that a patch actually reduced abuse.
